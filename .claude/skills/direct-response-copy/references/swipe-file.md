### ✅ Example: Liquid Death | Murder Your Thirst
**Date:** 2026-02-12
**Source:** https://liquiddeath.com/en-nl?srsltid=AfmBOoqglQz2-VzehOJxlYDSxuIoFYS6gZUtCITZQaQG35yp0Q0Rdk5H
**Type:** Web Page

**Why I saved this:**
I like the guarantee to not expose your head copy and how simple that is. I also like how simple this is, but also how funny it is, and how it makes you laugh a little bit.

#### Extracted Content
**Headline:** Murder Your Thirst
**CTA:** SHOP NOW

**Body Copy:**
```
GUARANTEED TO NOT EXPLODE YOUR HEAD. LESS SUGAR. HUGE FLAVOR. DEADLY MOUNTAINS. DELICIOUS WATER.
```
**Key Phrases:** "Murder your thirst", "guaranteed to not explode your head", "death to plastic bottles", "aluminum is infinitely recyclable"

#### Analysis
**Why it works:**
The use of humor and bold language effectively engages the audience, making the brand memorable while clearly communicating its core messages of being a better-for-you beverage choice. This is further heightened by the demand for ecological consciousness, particularly the strong stance against plastic waste.

**Target Audience:** Health-conscious consumers, environmentally-aware individuals, and younger demographics that appreciate humor and irreverence in branding.
**Tone & Voice:** Playful, irreverent, and confident; the playful language inverts the norms of beverage marketing.
**Emotional Triggers:** Humor, Health awareness, Environmental responsibility, Curiosity
**Techniques Used:** Humor, Shock value, Bold claims, Clear benefit statements

#### Key Takeaways
- Humor can be a highly effective emotional trigger in marketing when aligned with brand identity.
- Simplifying messaging while embedding deeper ecological and health-conscious themes increases relatability and impact.

### ✅ Example: Spend Management, Corporate Cards & Accounts Payable Solutions | Ramp
**Date:** 2026-02-12
**Source:** https://ramp.com/
**Type:** Web Page

**Why I saved this:**
I really like in the hero section:
- Specifically the video that's playing in the background
- The copy of "Multiply What's Possible"
- The fact that they don't draw too much attention with copy on the page
It's very simple, there's a video in the background. Let's play and it catches your eye immediately. The copy correlates with the video in the background.

#### Extracted Content
**Headline:** Multiply what’s possible
**CTA:** Get started for free

**Body Copy:**
```
Automated finance software that chases receipts, finds savings, and helps enforce policy.
```
**Key Phrases:** "50", "000+ finance teams have saved millions of hours with Ramp.", "Ramp AI", "Expenses that submit themselves", "Process bills in seconds", "Time is money. Save both."

#### Analysis
**Why it works:**
The effective combination of a powerful headline paired with a succinct body copy promotes a clear understanding of what's being offered while not overwhelming the user. The use of a minimalistic approach with selective copy and engaging visuals (background video) stimulates interest and encourages users to explore further.

**Target Audience:** Finance teams in organizations ranging from startups to large enterprises looking to streamline their expense management and finance operations.
**Tone & Voice:** Professional and confident, yet approachable and relatable. The tone conveys a sense of assurance that users can trust Ramp to solve their financial management problems.
**Emotional Triggers:** Efficiency, Time-saving, Overcoming frustration with finance tasks, Desire for simplicity and control, Curiosity about innovative solutions
**Techniques Used:** Social proof (reference to 50, 000+ finance teams), Simplicity in messaging, Use of numbers (savings, time saved) to instill trust, Descriptive language to evoke imagery and interest

#### Key Takeaways
- Effective use of multimedia (video) can enhance engagement and draw attention.
- A powerful headline combined with clear value propositions leads to higher conversion potential.
