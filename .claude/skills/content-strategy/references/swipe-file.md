## Content Pillar Examples

### Gong: "Show Don't Tell"
**Pillars:**
1. **Data-driven insights** - Original research from millions of sales calls
2. **Contrarian takes** - Challenge conventional sales wisdom
3. **Practical tactics** - Specific scripts and techniques that work

**Why it works:**
- Every piece is backed by proprietary data
- Makes bold claims competitors can't make
- Genuinely useful for practitioners

---

### Buffer: "Radical Transparency"
**Pillars:**
1. **Open salaries** - Published all employee salaries publicly
2. **Revenue transparency** - Monthly revenue reports
3. **Journey sharing** - The good, bad, and ugly of building a company

**Why it works:**
- Completely unique positioning
- Builds trust through vulnerability
- Creates content competitors literally can't copy

---

### Notion: "Build in Public"
**Pillars:**
1. **Templates library** - User-generated, curated templates
2. **Use case showcases** - How teams actually use Notion
3. **Community spotlights** - Celebrating power users

**Why it works:**
- Leverages community as content creators
- Shows product value through real examples
- Creates network effects

## Editorial Calendar Examples

### Content Marketing Institute Monthly Themes

| Month | Theme | Focus |
|-------|-------|-------|
| January | Planning & Strategy | Annual planning content |
| February | Audience & Personas | Know your audience |
| March | Content Ideation | Where ideas come from |
| April | Content Creation | Production best practices |
| May | Distribution | Amplification strategies |
| June | Measurement | Analytics and ROI |

**Why it works:**
- Logical progression through content lifecycle
- Allows deep dives vs. scattered topics
- Easy to plan ahead

---

### B2B SaaS Seasonal Calendar Pattern

| Quarter | Theme | Content Focus |
|---------|-------|---------------|
| Q1 | New Year Planning | Strategy guides, planning templates, trend predictions |
| Q2 | Implementation | How-to guides, case studies, tutorials |
| Q3 | Optimization | Performance improvement, efficiency, scaling |
| Q4 | Review & Prep | Year in review, budget justification, next year planning |

**Why it works:**
- Aligns with B2B buying cycles
- Budget planning in Q4 → buying in Q1
- Natural content themes

## Audience-Content Mapping Examples

### Slack's Multi-Audience Approach

| Audience | Content Type | Channel | Goal |
|----------|--------------|---------|------|
| End users (ICs) | Tips, tricks, productivity hacks | Blog, Twitter | Adoption |
| Team leads | Workflow templates, team case studies | Blog, Email | Expansion |
| IT/Admin | Security docs, compliance guides | Website, Webinars | Approval |
| Executives | ROI studies, industry reports | LinkedIn, Sales | Purchase |

**Why it works:**
- Different content for different buying committee roles
- Matches content to where each audience actually is
- Supports full funnel

## KPI Framework Examples

### Animalz Content Metrics Hierarchy

**Leading Indicators:**
- Content velocity (pieces published)
- Keyword rankings (positions)
- Backlinks acquired

**Engagement Indicators:**
- Time on page
- Scroll depth
- Pages per session

**Business Indicators:**
- MQLs from content
- Pipeline influenced
- Revenue attributed

**Why it works:**
- Leading indicators predict future success
- Engagement validates content quality
- Business indicators prove ROI

## Distribution Strategy Examples

### Ross Simmonds' Foundation Remix Strategy

**Original:** 3,000-word blog post on B2B content distribution

**Remixed into:**
1. Twitter thread (10 tweets) - Day 1
2. LinkedIn post (story format) - Day 2
3. LinkedIn carousel (10 slides) - Day 3
4. Newsletter feature - Week 1
5. YouTube video - Week 2
6. Podcast episode - Week 3
7. Webinar - Month 1
8. Conference talk - Quarter

**Result:** One piece of content → 8 content assets → 6+ months of distribution
