# Legends: Positioning Angles

Expert frameworks and methodologies that inform messaging angles and persuasion.

## Primary Experts

### Eugene Schwartz
**Framework:** Breakthrough Advertising
**Key Concepts:**
- 5 Levels of Market Awareness (Unaware → Most Aware)
- Market Sophistication stages
- Intensifying desire vs. creating it
- The power of the first line

**How we apply it:**
Match messaging angle to audience awareness level. Don't sell to unaware audiences—educate first.

---

### Gary Halbert
**Framework:** The Boron Letters / Hooks & Emotional Triggers
**Key Concepts:**
- "A-pile" vs "B-pile" mail (stand out or get ignored)
- Starving crowd principle
- Emotional triggers: fear, greed, curiosity, exclusivity
- The importance of the lead/hook

**How we apply it:**
Every angle needs a hook. Test emotional triggers. Lead with the most compelling element.

---

### Gary Bencivenga
**Framework:** Persuasion Principles
**Key Concepts:**
- Make it about them, not you
- Promise a transformation
- Use specificity (numbers, details)
- Create belief through proof
- Make the future vivid

**How we apply it:**
Every angle should promise transformation and use specific proof.

---

### Claude Hopkins
**Framework:** Scientific Advertising
**Key Concepts:**
- Test everything
- Specificity sells
- Reason-why advertising
- Track and measure

**How we apply it:**
Create multiple angles, test them, let data decide winners.

---

### Robert Cialdini
**Framework:** 7 Principles of Persuasion
**Key Concepts:**

**1. Reciprocity**
- Give value first, receive later
- Free guides, samples, insights create obligation
- Application: Lead magnets, free tools, valuable content

**2. Commitment & Consistency**
- Small yes leads to bigger yes
- People act in line with prior commitments
- Application: Micro-conversions, surveys, free trials

**3. Social Proof**
- People follow what others do
- Testimonials, reviews, user counts
- Application: "Join 10,000+ customers", case studies

**4. Authority**
- Experts are trusted
- Credentials, endorsements, media logos
- Application: "Featured in...", expert quotes, certifications

**5. Liking**
- People buy from those they like
- Similarity, compliments, familiarity
- Application: Brand personality, relatable messaging

**6. Scarcity**
- Limited = valuable
- Time limits, quantity limits, exclusive access
- Application: "Only 3 spots left", "Offer ends tonight"

**7. Unity**
- Shared identity creates trust
- "People like us do things like this"
- Application: Tribal language, us vs. them positioning

**How we apply it:**
Layer multiple principles into messaging. Use social proof + authority together. Scarcity only when genuine.

### Cognitive Biases for Marketing

**Loss Aversion**
- Losses hurt 2x more than gains feel good
- Frame as "Don't miss out" vs "Get this"
- Application: Risk-reversal, money-back guarantees

**Anchoring**
- First number becomes reference point
- Show higher price first, then actual price
- Application: "Was $500, now $197"

**Confirmation Bias**
- People seek info that confirms beliefs
- Align with existing worldviews
- Application: "You already know [problem]. Here's the solution."

**Bandwagon Effect**
- People do what others do
- Show momentum and popularity
- Application: "Fastest-growing", user counts, trending

**The IKEA Effect**
- People value what they help create
- Involvement increases ownership
- Application: Quizzes, configurators, personalization

## Persuasion Sequencing

**When to use which principle:**

| Stage | Best Principles |
|-------|-----------------|
| Cold/Unaware | Social proof, Authority, Curiosity |
| Problem Aware | Loss aversion, Commitment (small ask) |
| Solution Aware | Social proof, Scarcity, Unity |
| Product Aware | Authority, Reciprocity, Liking |
| Most Aware | Scarcity, Commitment, Direct offer |
