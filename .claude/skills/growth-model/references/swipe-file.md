### ✅ Example: Spend Management, Corporate Cards & Accounts Payable Solutions | Ramp
**Date:** 2026-02-12
**Source:** https://ramp.com/
**Type:** Web Page

**Why I saved this:**
Like how simple the hero section copy is of the Multiply website: "What's possible," with very little text and the video in the background.

#### Extracted Content
**Headline:** Multiply what’s possible
**Subheadline:** Automated finance software that chases receipts, finds savings, and helps enforce policy.
**CTA:** Get started for free

**Body Copy:**
```
Ramp's spend management platform simplifies expense management with corporate cards, travel, expenses, and accounts payable to improve efficiency and automate finance operations.
```
**Key Phrases:** "50", "000+ finance teams", "saved millions of hours", "expenses that submit themselves", "process bills in seconds", "let Ramp automate"

#### Analysis
**Why it works:**
The simplicity of the hero section, paired with a powerful headline, effectively grabs attention and clearly communicates the main benefit of the product. The use of quantifiable social proof (e.g., 50,000+ finance teams saved millions of hours) builds trust and credibility, encouraging potential customers to take action.

**Target Audience:** Finance teams and decision-makers in various industries that seek to streamline and automate their financial operations, as well as CTOs and CFOs looking for efficiency in expense management.
**Tone & Voice:** Professional yet approachable, highlighting efficiency while maintaining an encouraging and supportive tone.
**Emotional Triggers:** Trust, Curiosity, Relief from financial management burdens, Desire for efficiency
**Techniques Used:** Social proof, Quantitative claims, Specific benefits, Clear calls-to-action, Urgency in implementation

#### Key Takeaways
- Simplicity in messaging can effectively communicate value and drive user engagement.
- Incorporating social proof through numbers and testimonials builds credibility and encourages conversions.

### ✅ Example: Liquid Death | Murder Your Thirst

**CTA:** SHOP NOW

**Body Copy:**
```
GUARANTEED TO NOT EXPLODE YOUR HEAD. LESS SUGAR. HUGE FLAVOR. DEADLY MOUNTAINS. DELICIOUS WATER.
```
**Key Phrases:** "Murder your thirst", "guaranteed to not explode your head", "death to plastic bottles", "aluminum is infinitely recyclable"

#### Analysis
**Why it works:**
The use of humor and bold language effectively engages the audience, making the brand memorable while clearly communicating its core messages of being a better-for-you beverage choice. This is further heightened by the demand for ecological consciousness, particularly the strong stance against plastic waste.

**Target Audience:** Health-conscious consumers, environmentally-aware individuals, and younger demographics that appreciate humor and irreverence in branding.
**Tone & Voice:** Playful, irreverent, and confident; the playful language inverts the norms of beverage marketing.
**Emotional Triggers:** Humor, Health awareness, Environmental responsibility, Curiosity
**Techniques Used:** Humor, Shock value, Bold claims, Clear benefit statements

#### Key Takeaways
- Humor can be a highly effective emotional trigger in marketing when aligned with brand identity.
- Simplifying messaging while embedding deeper ecological and health-conscious themes increases relatability and impact.

### ✅ Example: Spend Management, Corporate Cards & Accounts Payable Solutions | Ramp
**Date:** 2026-02-12
**Source:** https://ramp.com/
**Type:** Web Page

**Why I saved this:**
I love the hero section of the Multiply website. What's possible and how simple it is, and how good the video is in the background.

#### Extracted Content
**Headline:** Automated finance software that chases receipts, finds savings, and helps enforce policy.
**Subheadline:** Make expense management easy with Ramp's spend management platform.
**CTA:** Get started for free

**Body Copy:**
```
Replace multiple broken tools with Ramp, the only platform designed to make your finance team faster—and happier. Control spend before it happens, and let expenses submit themselves.
```
**Key Phrases:** "50", "000+ finance teams have saved millions of hours with Ramp.", "Set policies that enforce themselves.", "Expenses that submit themselves.", "Leave the busywork to us."

#### Analysis
**Why it works:**
The content effectively combines clear, compelling messaging with a strong demonstration of benefits to the user. It integrates social proof, emphasizes ease of use and time savings, and presents a robust value proposition, catering to the needs of finance teams seeking efficiency in expense management.

**Target Audience:** The primary audience appears to be finance professionals and teams in businesses of all sizes, particularly those looking to streamline their financial processes and reduce administrative overhead.
**Tone & Voice:** Professional but approachable; the tone communicates confidence and reliability while remaining relatable, which resonates well with busy professionals.
**Emotional Triggers:** Relief from tedious processes, Desire for efficiency and accuracy, Empowerment through automation, Trust from social proof
**Techniques Used:** Social proof through testimonials, Urgency by suggesting quick implementation timelines, Specificity with data points (e.g., '50, 000+ finance teams'), Benefits-focused messaging

#### Key Takeaways
- Highlighting user testimonials can significantly enhance trust and credibility.
- Presenting data-driven benefits and ease of use attracts target audiences more effectively.
